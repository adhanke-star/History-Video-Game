## 1. The decision in one page

**UNLOCKED BUT JUDGED.** Gameplay features are never removed on moral grounds; the
game's moral teaching voice is never removed for gameplay's sake.

- **The toggle stays** (Aaron: "restore mayhem/historical mode toggle"): Historical =
  plausible Civil War simulation; Mayhem = full-authority timeline sandbox, unchanged.
  Picker behavior unchanged (explicit choice, fail-closed Historical).
- **Historical unlocks mechanics:** surrender/no-quarter/atrocity actions PLAYABLE with
  real simulated effects — **judged, never rewarded**: the AAR and infamy ledger condemn
  them; victory scoring never adds points for massacre.
- **The moral voice stays:** graded AAR (performance AND moral framing, the
  anti-Lost-Cause cause panel), ending verdicts, infamy condemnation. Mayhem keeps its
  chartered no-moral-GPA identity.
- **The teaching companion goes always-visible in BOTH modes** ("In history…" sourced
  panels + Chronicle juxtaposition; amends D416's off-by-default comparison).
- **NEW: the CONQUEST layer** — a Risk-style operational campaign (§5) makes
  player-authored alt-history a played system, not a menu choice.

## 5. THE CONQUEST LAYER — Risk-style player-authored alt-history (R9-R11)

**Thesis:** break the rung system. The campaign becomes a map you play ON, not a
ladder you climb. Both rulesets get it: Historical conquest = plausibility-railed
operational freedom (the war can diverge for REASONS — that's teachable alt-history);
Mayhem conquest = the full sandbox.

1. **Map:** BOTH layers (Aaron's pick, scope-flagged): a named-territory strategic
   graph (~30-45 territories; states/sub-theaters) as the Risk board, with hex zoom-in
   for operational detail (subsumes the parked Phase D item). Adjacency = rail, river,
   road, and sea links.
2. **Movement is transport-real (Aaron's addition):** army movement capacity rides the
   RAIL system (the logistics-rail layer exists), rivers, roads, and NAVAL TRANSFER
   along coasts/rivers (ties the naval packet in; McClellan's Peninsula move as the
   model). Cutting rails/rivers cuts movement — the raids tie-in.
3. **Risk levers:** territory reinforcements (held territories feed manpower/supply
   through the existing economy) · theater bonuses (Mississippi Valley, Eastern
   seaboard, blockade coast — teaches why theaters mattered) · free army
   movement/attack along adjacencies · objective cards/set turn-ins adapted as war-
   department directives tied to political capital + loot.
4. **Resolution:** ALWAYS playable, ALWAYS optional — any engagement can drop into the
   full tactical battle; auto-resolve (existing pipeline + Q7 matchup odds) carries
   the rest at the player's choice.
5. **The Council of War** (pre-battle phase, conquest AND ladder): choose the battle
   SITE (terrain preview, high-ground read), troop commitment, equipment via the
   armory, doctrine — all D74-clean inputs through the existing conditioning bridge.
   "Pick the high ground to fight even if not historical."
6. **Terrain: real ground, period surface, $0 licenses.** Google Maps ruled OUT
   (license-incompatible with the public $0 build). Pipeline: USGS public-domain DEMs
   (3DEP/SRTM) give real elevation for any site; Library of Congress Civil War maps
   (Hotchkiss et al.) + NPS battlefield maps (public domain) guide 1860s land cover
   (woods, fields, fords, roads). PRE-BAKED at build time for a curated set of
   plausible battle sites per territory (fords, gaps, rail junctions, ridgelines);
   compressed into the data layer so the single offline HTML holds.
7. **Alt-history juxtaposition rides it:** every conquest divergence gets the sourced
   "In history…" companion + Chronicle fork tracking (§4a.2, the R2 player-authored
   divergence lock); counterfactual seminar content under the citation law.

### 8.2 Evidence versus gameplay architecture

Evidence may establish a dated place, physical connection, interchange, navigation
condition, chokepoint, cut, repair, reopening, operation, or explicit non-link. It may
also establish uncertainty. Evidence does **not** choose:

- a territory or hex boundary;
- whether a place is a territory, node, edge endpoint, site, port state, or label;
- the campaign clock, control-state machine, route ownership, or divergence rule;
- movement points, capacity, cost, reinforcement, supply, economy, repair, objective,
  AI, UI, save, or battle-conditioning values;
- whether rail, river, road, and sea share one runtime schema; or
- a historical outcome for a player-authored timeline.

The final law must preserve `Verified` / `Inferred` / `Disputed`, exact dates,
source-family independence, conditional reach, closures/reopenings, and explicit
non-links. A visually convenient connection cannot outrank the packet. Historical
record and `Your Timeline` state must remain separately labelled.

### 8.5 Required decision process

The territory decision begins with candidate maps, not a single assumed graph. Each
candidate must stay inside D455's approximate 30-45 envelope and be scored against:

- rail/water packet coverage and explicit non-links;
- historical/operational teaching value across eastern, western, coastal, border, and
  Trans-Mississippi areas without forced symmetry;
- legible keyboard- and screen-reader-navigable presentation at the target viewport;
- Council-of-War site inheritance and the difference between a strategic territory,
  transport node, and tactical site;
- public-domain/offline asset size and pre-bake cost; and
- the smallest state/save surface that can fail closed without poisoning ladder play.

For time and control, the design session must compare at least: a coarse turn-owned
state; an event/delta ledger over a persistent graph; and a hybrid with historically
dated availability plus player-authored control receipts. The comparison must show
how each option handles opening dates, cuts, closures, repairs, captures, bypasses,
seasonal/conditional reach, and a divergent campaign without rewriting canonical
history. No option may turn a historical date into a predetermined game outcome.

For the common transport interface, the law must answer whether evidence categories
share an envelope while mode-specific facts remain separate, or whether rail, water,
road, and sea stay separate registries joined only through explicit interchange rows.
It must define referential integrity, absent/malformed fallback, date/control
authority, non-link representation, and double-count prevention before any schema is
created. Packet compatibility is not permission to join them.
