**Status:** D327 durable battle-build research packet (docs only; no data/runtime/registry change).

This lane's whole point is dignified Black combat agency and anti-Lost-Cause truth: build the fights where USCT valor is documentable and playable, teach the injustices (pay, white-officer policy, no-quarter, the Crater pull-out) as framing, and hold a hard dignity line that a massacre (Fort Pillow) is NEVER a gamified playable win. New Market Heights is the strongest ready-to-spec battle-build; Fort Pillow is teaching-only.

## 2. Recommended Playable Shape

**Top candidate - New Market Heights / Chaffin's Farm (T8 two-phase, attacker = US).** The USCT story is the assault on the heights; the fuller Chaffin's Farm day also included the XVIII Corps storming Fort Harrison. Recommended:
- **Phase 1 - New Market Heights (scoreWeight 3, decisive).** Objective: Paine's USCT division storms the abatis-fronted heights along New Market Road. Attacker-must-seize per the engine grain: give the USCT a real strength mass, model the obstacle belt as dense cover/slowing terrain, fog OFF (this is a foggy-dawn attacker-must-win fight - use woods+abatis density, not fog, per the D90 lesson that fog aids the defender), and script the thin CS defense (Gregg's Texas Brigade, plus Gary's dismounted cavalry) to be pried loose as the Fort Harrison threat draws it off (timed defender-thinning, not a damage buff).
- **Phase 2 - Fort Harrison (scoreWeight 1).** Objective: XVIII Corps carries the fort on Chaffin's Bluff. Broadens the field, but casualties/score carry forward from the decisive USCT phase.

Because carrying the heights against works is genuinely hard in an objective-hold engine, the decisive Phase 1 must lean US via true inputs (strength edge + timed CS withdrawal + open final approach once the abatis is passed), never a per-battle attacker buff. Alternatively ship New Market Heights as a **single-phase** assault if the Fort Harrison OOB proves too thin.

**Second candidate - Fort Wagner (single-phase, attacker = US, historical lean = CS/repulse).** A twilight assault down a 1,200-yard beach neck against an entrenched fort. The engine-honest design: narrow approach (marsh+surf channel the marker), strong CS defenders on a hill work, fog OFF. The probe must ALLOW and expect a repulse lean - the teaching is valor and casualty ratio under fire (roughly 40-45% loss in the 54th - over 250 of ~600 engaged), not a captured fort. This is the lane's proof that a heroic FAILURE is a legitimate, dignified build.

## 6. Teaching Cards And Anti-Lost-Cause Framing

1. **"The question is settled."** New Market Heights + Nashville: 14 Medals of Honor and the line traditionally attributed to Maj. Gen. George H. Thomas over the USCT dead ("the question is settled; negroes will fight" - a memoir-attributed quotation, not a contemporaneous order; cite the attribution) document Black combat capacity against the era's racist doubt. Frame as agency, not as white validation.
2. **The Crater injustice.** Ferrero's USCT division trained for weeks to lead the assault; Meade, backed by Grant, pulled them the day before over political optics, substituting an unprepared white division whose commanders (Ledlie, Ferrero) hid in a bunker drinking. Name the command failure plainly - the disaster flowed from that decision, and USCT sent in late were then targeted.
3. **No quarter / Fort Pillow.** Confederate forces under Maj. Gen. Forrest massacred surrendering Black soldiers (Britannica: ~200 of ~300 Union dead were Black; Wikipedia: only ~20% of Black troops taken prisoner vs ~60% of white). DIGNITY RULE: this is a teaching/memory card ONLY - never a scenario, objective, or "win." The card names it a massacre and a war crime, centers the victims, and refuses any Lost-Cause softening of Forrest.
4. **Unequal pay and the white-officer policy.** USCT were paid less than white soldiers until 1864 (the 54th Mass refused all pay in protest); Black men were barred from commissions as line officers with rare exceptions (Cailloux among the first). Teach these as the injustices the soldiers fought and served under simultaneously.
5. **Valor in defeat.** Fort Wagner and Olustee were Union failures where USCT fought with distinction (54th Mass ~40-45% loss; USCT covering the Olustee retreat). Teach that the dignity is in the fighting and sacrifice, not a captured objective - the game must not invent a victory to feel good.

## 7. D74 No-Fudge Risks

The specific temptation in THIS lane: an implementer wanting the USCT to "win their moment" will be tempted to give USCT markers a per-battle valor/damage buff so New Market Heights carries, or to force the 54th over the Fort Wagner parapet, or to script Fort Pillow casualties. ALL forbidden. The correct levers:
- New Market Heights seize must emerge from **true OOB strength mass + open final approach after the obstacle belt + timed CS withdrawal to meet the Fort Harrison threat**, never a fireMult or attacker buff.
- Fort Wagner and Olustee must be allowed to **resolve as historical repulses/defeats** - do NOT add a casualtyMult or winner gate to manufacture a win; the teaching is the ratio and the valor, produced by narrow terrain + strong defenders.
- Nashville Peach Orchard Hill's value is that the assault is **repulsed yet pins the CS line** - model the pin via reinforcement/timing pressure on the objective, not a hidden score bonus.
- No "heroism" stat, no `valorMult`, no `fudge`, no per-battle casualty floor. The dignity is honored by TRUE inputs (accurate counts, terrain, timing) under the one universal model, exactly per D92.
