The final nine days of the Army of Northern Virginia are the lane's spine. Five Forks (Apr 1) is a clean flank attack on an entrenched, thinly held refused line with a decapitated command - the "Waterloo of the Confederacy" - and is the lead buildable candidate as a single-phase attacker-seize. This lane ADJOINS the existing 1864-65-attrition packet - the boundary is stated below.

## 2. Recommended Playable Shape

**Five Forks (top candidate) - single-phase attacker-seize (RECOMMENDED primary).**
- Roles: attacker = US ("US"), defender = CS ("CS"). US must seize the Five Forks crossroads by turning the refused CS left ("the return"/the Angle) along White Oak Road.
- Objective-hold fit: give the CS defender the entrenched log works along White Oak Road but a THIN, refused left flank and NO army-commander bonus in the decisive sector (Pickett, Fitz Lee, and Rosser were away at Rosser's shad bake, an acoustic shadow hiding the fighting); give US ~2:1 bodies (Sheridan's dismounted cavalry fixing the front + Warren's V Corps swinging onto the return). The historic US break-in EMERGES from the strength edge + the undermanned refused flank + the absent command - not a switch. Fog OFF (late-afternoon assault; the CS problem was a bad refused line and absent generals, not visibility).
- **Optional T8 two-phase enhancement:** Phase 1 "Sheridan's Cavalry Fixes the Front" (scoreWeight 1) - dismounted cavalry pins the CS center/right; Phase 2 "Warren's V Corps Turns the Return" (scoreWeight 3, DECISIVE) - V Corps rolls up the refused left and takes the crossroads. Weights sum to 4 (1 + 3 DECISIVE).

**Third Petersburg / Apr 2 Breakthrough (second candidate) - T8 two-phase, US attacker both phases.**
- Phase 1 "The VI Corps Breakthrough - Boydton Plank Road" (scoreWeight 3, DECISIVE): Wright's massed VI Corps ruptures a stretched, thinned CS line before dawn; US attacker-seize. A.P. Hill is killed riding to rally his line (teaching-card beat, NOT a scripted death mechanic).
- Phase 2 "The Stand at Fort Gregg" (scoreWeight 1): ~500-600 CS (Mississippi/NC/GA fragments + artillery) hold Fort Gregg and Fort Whitworth against Gibbon's XXIV Corps to buy time for the Petersburg/Richmond evacuation; US eventually seizes but pays heavily. Weights sum to 4 (3 DECISIVE + 1). This is an honorable last stand - fine to build; not a massacre.

**Sailor's Creek (third candidate) - single-phase attacker-seize (optional).** US (Wright's VI Corps + Sheridan's cavalry incl. Custer) assaults the trapped Ewell/Anderson rearguard at Marshall's Crossroads and the Hillsman farm; CS is enveloped. Encode the lopsided US strength + the trapped/no-escape geometry and let the collapse emerge; the mass capture and the captured generals are teaching text, never a count-forcing gate.

**Boundary with the 1864-65-attrition packet (no double-claim):** that packet owns the Overland Campaign, Cold Harbor, the Petersburg SIEGE proper (Jun 1864-Mar 1865), and the Crater. It lists the "Petersburg final breakthrough (Apr 2 1865)" only as rank-8 Med-Low and EXPLICITLY defers it ("entirely un-researched here; keep teaching-only until a dedicated pass"). This packet claims and researches the Apr 2 Breakthrough as the opening of the Appomattox Campaign. Fort Stedman (Mar 25 1865) is a gray-zone last-CS-offensive that could go to either packet; recommend it stay a teaching card for now to avoid overlap.

## 4. OOB And Rank Traps

- **Ulysses S. Grant - Lieutenant General, US** (revived grade, commissioned Mar 10 1864). CORRECT here, unlike the pre-Oct-1862 lanes; do NOT downgrade him to Maj. Gen. He directs via Meade; he is NOT a full "General" (four-star grade created for him only July 1866). ABT-confirmed "Lieutenant General."
- **Philip H. Sheridan - Major General, US**, commanding the combined cavalry + attached V Corps at Five Forks and the pursuit force at Sailor's Creek. Not a lieutenant general. ABT-confirmed "Maj. Gen." (repo generals.json entry us-sheridan already ships "Five Forks" in his bio - reuse it.)
- **Gouverneur K. Warren - Major General, US, V Corps - the lane's headline command trap.** Sheridan RELIEVED him of command DURING Five Forks (Apr 1 1865) over the timing of the flank assault. Warren's court of inquiry (convened by President Hayes, Special Orders No. 277, Dec 9 1879; verdict Nov 1882) found Sheridan's relief unjustified - a POSTHUMOUS vindication (Warren died Aug 8 1882, months before the Nov 1882 findings; all three dates CONFIRMED this pass). Any Five Forks OOB must model Warren commanding V Corps at the START and hand V Corps to **Charles Griffin** at the moment of relief; do not leave Warren in command through the whole fight, and do not erase the relief (it is a major teaching card).

## 6. Teaching Cards And Anti-Lost-Cause Framing

- **"Warren's Ruin - and His Vindication."** Sheridan relieved a competent corps commander in the flush of victory; it took a court of inquiry convened 14 years later, reporting in 1882 AFTER Warren's death, to clear him. Teach command injustice and the slowness of institutional redress - not a villain/hero binary.
- **"The Shad Bake, Carefully."** Pickett, Fitz Lee, and Rosser were away eating fish behind the lines while their command was overrun, an acoustic shadow hiding the gunfire. Teach it as a documented command failure, but flag that the exact details are contested and the anecdote has been used to caricature Pickett - present it as evidence of a decapitated command, not as folklore.
- **"The Line Breaks Because It Was Stretched."** Apr 2 was not Confederate cowardice; Lee's works were manned at a fraction of the density they needed after ten months of siege attrition. The rupture EMERGES from thin manpower vs a massed assault - the honest cause, not a Lost-Cause "overwhelmed by numbers alone" gloss that omits why the numbers were lopsided (emancipation and Northern manpower/industry that the Confederacy's slave society could not match).
- **"USCT at the End."** The XXV Corps (United States Colored Troops) blocked the Confederacy's last breakout at Appomattox and were among the first into Richmond. Black soldiers helped FORCE the surrender - a fact the later reconciliation narrative erased. Center Black agency; the freedmen's presence on the field is the emancipationist meaning of Appomattox.
- **"The Surrender Is Not a 'Gentleman's Agreement' Myth."** Grant's generous terms were sound policy, but the later celebration of mutual white respect at Appomattox fed a reconciliationist memory (name **David Blight, Race and Reunion, 2001**) that reunited North and South around white sectional healing AT THE EXPENSE of Black freedom and the emancipationist cause. Teach the surrender as the end of the slaveholders' rebellion, not as a chivalric handshake. DIGNITY LINE: the surrender is a teaching sequence, never a scored battle for either side.
