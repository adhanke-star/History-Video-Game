# Western Theater Gaps (Perryville + Stones River) - Battle-Build Research Packet

**Status:** Durable battle-build research packet (docs only; no data/runtime/registry change).
**Lane:** western-gaps
**Verdict:** READY_FOR_SPEC

The two big Western fights the game skipped between Shiloh (built) and Chickamauga (built). Stones River / Murfreesboro (Dec 31 1862 - Jan 2 1863) is the lead: a natural TWO-combat-phase T8 (the Dec 31 dawn attack and Round Forest stand, then Breckinridge's Jan 2 repulse across the river, with the Jan 1 lull as a teaching interstitial, not a scored phase). Perryville / Chaplin Hills (Oct 8 1862) is a strong single-phase second. Both carry a rare, honest teaching truth this project should get right: NEITHER is a lopsided Fredericksburg-style defender win. Both are bloody, near-parity fights the Union won STRATEGICALLY (the enemy withdrew), not by a casualty differential - so the D74 no-fudge path here is the opposite of the usual "the attacker gets slaughtered" pattern, and the packet's Section 7 exists mainly to stop an implementer from forcing US losses below CS.

## 2. Recommended Playable Shape

**Stones River (top candidate) - T8 TWO-phase, CS attacker / US defender both phases.**
- Roles: attacker = CS ("CS"), defender = US ("US") in both phases. Rosecrans's Army of the Cumberland is the objective-holder.
- **Phase 1 "December 31 - The Dawn Attack & Round Forest" (scoreWeight 3, DECISIVE).** At 6:00 a.m. Hardee's and Polk's corps smash the unready Union right (McCook's wing) and wheel it back like a jackknife toward the Nashville Pike; the US line bends but anchors on the Round Forest ("Hell's Half Acre") and the Nashville Pike / railroad interior line and does NOT break. Objective: US holds the Nashville Pike corridor and the Round Forest. Historical lean: US holds (this is where the battle was decided). This is the DECISIVE phase.
- **Phase 2 "January 2 - Breckinridge's Assault at McFadden's Ford" (scoreWeight 1).** ~4:00 p.m., ~5,000 Confederates cross half a mile of open field against the US-held high ground east of Stones River and are torn apart by massed Union guns under Capt. John Mendenhall firing from the west bank (the fetched ABT page says "nearly 50 cannon hub-to-hub"; secondary accounts count ~57-58 - teaching flavor, NOT an engine input; the universal artillery/canister model, not a hard gun count, produces the repulse). Objective: US holds the high ground / the massed battery line. Historical lean: US holds, sharply. scoreWeights sum = **4** (3 DECISIVE + 1); this is a TWO-phase scenario, NOT three, so it sums to 4, not 5. The Jan 1 lull is a teaching interstitial between the two phases, never its own scored phase.
- Engine-grain fit: US wins BOTH holds because it keeps more bodies on the objective plus terrain - the Round Forest cedars + the interior Nashville Pike/railroad line on Dec 31, and the massed-artillery west bank + open half-mile approach on Jan 2 (the universal canister/artillery model does the Jan 2 repulse on its own). Do NOT lean on a casualty differential; see Section 7.
- Fog: recommend fog OFF for both phases. The Dec 31 dawn surprise must EMERGE from accurate positioning (CS concentrated attacking mass hitting an unentrenched, un-refused US right at breakfast), not from a fog flag that artificially aids the attacker; Jan 2 is an open-field assault the gunners plainly saw coming. (A dawn-mist fog-ON option for Phase 1 is defensible but flagged in Section 9 - it must not become the mechanism that manufactures the CS gains.)

**Perryville (second candidate) - single-phase objective-hold, CS attacker / US defender.**
- Roles: attacker = CS ("CS"), defender = US ("US"). The US objective-holder is McCook's ENGAGED wing (plus the stabilizing brigades), NOT the whole 55,000-man Army of the Ohio.
- Objective: US holds the Chaplin Hills line (Open Knob / Loomis's Heights / the Benton Road ridge / Dixville Crossroads) through the afternoon; CS (Cheatham's division leading) drives it back over a mile but does not shatter it. Historical lean: near-even, CS gains ground, US holds by evening and Bragg withdraws -> strategic US victory.
- Engine-grain fit + the acoustic shadow: the honest, no-fudge representation of "Buell could not hear his own battle" is simply that the unengaged Union corps are OFF the map - encode only the truly ENGAGED force as the US OOB (an accurate-input choice, D92), and carry the acoustic shadow as a teaching card explaining WHY the rest of the army never came up. No "reserves-locked" or morale-penalty switch. Fog OFF. This makes Perryville a roughly-even fight rather than a 55k-vs-16k blowout that never happened.

## 4. OOB And Rank Traps

- **THE OCT-1862 LIEUTENANT-GENERAL FLIP (the headline trap).** The CSA lieutenant-general grade did not exist before Oct 1862. **Leonidas Polk** and **William J. Hardee** were among the first Lt. Gens., effective **Oct 10 1862** (Polk's appointment announced Oct 11). Perryville was **Oct 8 1862** - TWO DAYS earlier. So at **Perryville both Polk and Hardee were MAJOR GENERALS**; at **Stones River (Dec 31 1862) both were LIEUTENANT GENERALS**. Encode the exact grade per battle date; the fetched ABT Stones River page independently confirms "Lt. Gen. William J. Hardee." Do not copy one battle's OOB to the other.
- **Braxton Bragg = General (full), C.S.A.** on BOTH dates (promoted full general Apr 12 1862). Commanding the **Army of the Mississippi** at Perryville and the **Army of Tennessee** at Stones River (he renamed it in Nov 1862). Both ABT pages label him "Gen."; correct. Do not down-rank him to Lt. Gen.
- **Don Carlos Buell = Major General, US** (Army of the Ohio) at Perryville. TRAP: the ABT page loosely writes "Gen. Don Carlos Buell" - he was a **Major General of Volunteers**, not a full general (no US full-general grade existed then). Encode Maj. Gen. **William S. Rosecrans was NOT at Perryville** - he replaced Buell in late Oct 1862; do not place him on the Perryville map.
- **William S. Rosecrans = Major General, US** (Army of the Cumberland) at Stones River. ABT-confirmed "Maj. Gen."
- **John C. Breckinridge = Major General, C.S.A.** at Stones River, division commander; he made the Jan 2 assault under protest. ABT-confirmed "Maj. Gen." Do not inflate to Lt. Gen.
- **Patrick R. Cleburne - RANK FLIP.** **Brigadier General** at Perryville (where he was wounded), **Major General** at Stones River (promoted **Dec 13 1862**, ~18 days before the battle). Do not carry MG back to Perryville.
- **Philip H. Sheridan - RANK TRAP.** He commanded a **division at BOTH** battles (Gilbert's III Corps at Perryville; McCook's right wing at Stones River, where his stand in the Cedars cost him all three brigade commanders, Sill killed). He was a **Brigadier General during both battles**; his **Major-General-of-Volunteers appointment (April 10 1863) carried a DATE OF RANK of Dec 31 1862 - awarded FOR Stones River** (the Thomas-at-Nashville pattern). The Dec 31 1862 date is a backdated date-of-rank, NOT the day he became a major general - so encode Brig. Gen. at both battles and do not backdate the MG onto either OOB. (Re-verified this pass - Section 9.)
- **James A. Garfield - off-field trap.** Garfield became Rosecrans's **chief of staff in early 1863, AFTER Stones River**; his famous ride is Chickamauga (Sept 1863). He was a **Brigadier General** but was **not a factor at Stones River** - do not place him on this lane's maps.
- **Benjamin F. Cheatham = Major General, C.S.A.** - his division led the main Confederate attack at Perryville (the Open Knob / Loomis's Heights assault). **Alexander M. McCook = Major General, US**, the corps whose isolated wing bore the Perryville fight and (a different wing) the Dec 31 collapse at Stones River.
- **Engaged-vs-present guard (both battles).** Perryville: ABT lists US 55,000 *present* but only ~22,000 *engaged* (the acoustic-shadow point) and CS 16,000 engaged (of Bragg's larger force in the area). Stones River: US 41,400 engaged. Encode the ENGAGED figures as the OOB, not the paper strength, or Perryville becomes an ahistorical blowout.

## 6. Teaching Cards And Anti-Lost-Cause Framing

- **"Won by Holding, Not by Killing."** The lane's core teaching. Stones River cost the Union MORE men than the Confederates in raw terms (US ~12,900 vs CS ~11,700) yet was a Union victory because Rosecrans held the field and Bragg retreated; Perryville cost the Union more (4,211 vs 3,401) on a flank the Confederates tactically won, yet Bragg abandoned Kentucky. Teach that a strategic victory can be bought with heavier losses - the opposite of the Fredericksburg lesson - and that "who won" is decided by who holds the ground and the campaign, not by the casualty scoreboard.
- **"The Acoustic Shadow."** Buell sat two miles from a battle he could not hear; an acoustic shadow (a real topographic/meteorological effect) muffled the sound so his veteran corps stood idle while McCook's men fought five hours alone. Frame it honestly: the shadow is real and ABT-attested, but it is ONE factor - Buell had also been injured in a fall from his horse and his staff coordination failed; do not over-attribute the whole Union passivity to acoustics. The card teaches how command-and-communication friction, not just enemy action, shapes a battle.
- **"Breckinridge's Protest."** On Jan 2 Breckinridge told Bragg the assault across open ground into massed Union guns would fail; ordered forward anyway, his ~5,000 men lost roughly 1,800 in about an hour. Teach it as a command decision and its predictable cost (the artillery arm's decisive defensive moment), not as doomed Southern gallantry.
- **"Bragg and His Generals."** After Stones River, Bragg circulated a round-robin demanding subordinates affirm confidence in him; the feud with Polk and Hardee that later helped lose Chattanooga starts here. Anti-Lost-Cause framing: the Army of Tennessee's dysfunction was internal command failure, consistent with the game's existing Bragg treatment (`data/generals.json`), not bad luck.
- No massacre or atrocity content in this lane; no dignity-line constraint applies.

## 7. D74 No-Fudge Risks

- **THE PARITY TEMPTATION (the biggest risk in this lane).** Because both battles are Union victories, an implementer may reflexively add a `casualtyMult`/`lossMult`/`killMult` to push US losses BELOW CS losses. FORBIDDEN, and here it is also HISTORICALLY WRONG - the US took MORE casualties at both battles. There is NO aggregate casualty differential to force. The US wins by HOLDING the objective (the Nashville Pike + Round Forest on Dec 31; the high ground + massed guns on Jan 2; the Chaplin Hills line at Perryville) with adequate bodies + terrain + interior lines. Encode true near-parity strengths and let the objective-hold model decide; the aggregate casualty-DIRECTION guard must be **near-parity (within a modest band), NOT US < CS**.
- **The Jan 2 exception.** The ONE phase with a genuinely lopsided direction is Breckinridge's assault: an open half-mile into ~50+ massed guns. That emerges on its own from the universal artillery/canister model + open approach + fog OFF - no per-phase switch. A phase-scoped guard "CS Phase-2 losses > US Phase-2 losses" is legitimate; the AGGREGATE-battle guard is not.
- **The acoustic-shadow temptation.** Do NOT invent a `fudge`/morale/"reserves-locked" flag to simulate Buell's deafness. The honest model is accurate inputs (D92): the unengaged Union corps are simply absent from the Perryville OOB, and the shadow is a teaching card. Zero combat parameters to tune.
- **The Dec 31 surprise temptation.** Do NOT fabricate a `powerMult`/`fireMult` "surprise bonus" for the dawn attack. The CS Dec 31 gains must emerge from accurate positioning - concentrated attacking mass against an un-refused, unentrenched US right caught at breakfast - and from the US NOT being dug in on that flank. Terrain + OOB + timing, never a per-battle key.
