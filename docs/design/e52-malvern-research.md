## Why this correction exists (the D252 evidence, in one paragraph)
The E48 complete-the-hold rule — proven at its Chancellorsville target — exposed that Malvern Hill's shipped US 8/8 was pure clock: every base win was `by=timeout`, and the buzzer diagnostic showed the CS mid-hold ON the Crew-house objective (5,286–13,814 men, US zero in the radius, 4–6 US units routing) on EVERY seed. The D240 panel had already recorded the calibration gap (post-flip atk/def 1.34–1.45 vs the historical 1.8–2.7 band) and deferred it. The modeled defense was ~2.6× light where it mattered most: the crest infantry.

## The two load-bearing findings
1. **The US crest infantry was erased.** Sears (To the Gates of Richmond, pp. 311–312) puts **~17,800 men of Morell's + Couch's divisions** on the northern face of the hill; a second (Sears/Crenshaw-lineage) account gives 17,500 "protecting the guns." The model had **6,800** for those two divisions. The ~17,800 is Morell+Couch ONLY — it excludes Sykes (west slope/River Road), Kearny/Hooker (III Corps, right/rear reserve, committed piecemeal), and the late brigades. Corrected US infantry (26,800) is consistent with American Battlefield Trust's **~27,000 US engaged** — a coherence check, though not fully independent (the Meagher add is itself part of what closes the gap; panel catch).
2. **The CS assault was also under-modeled — and is raised honestly (rule: accuracy, not outcome).** NPS + American Battlefield Trust independently give **~30,000 CS engaged** ("twenty brigades under seven division commanders"); the model had 20,800. The correction raises BOTH armies; the repulse must emerge from the gun line + the crest weight, not from starving the attacker.
