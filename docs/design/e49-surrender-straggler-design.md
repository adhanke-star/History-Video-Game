## §2 — The design law (the build milestone honors this verbatim)

- **SL-1 · STRAGGLER-SHEDDING.** On every rout EVENT — the T0:553 transition into `state="routing"` — the unit immediately and permanently sheds `round(u.men * 0.05)` men to the missing pool (`__FIELD.missing[side] += shed; u.men -= shed`). Deterministic, no RNG draw, fires at the moment `routEverCount` increments. **f = 0.05 is ONE universal documented constant:** a normal battle's 1–2 breaks/unit yields ~5–10% aggregate straggling (order-consistent with the Shiloh under-the-bluff literature, thousands of ~40,000 engaged); the D251 eroded traces (~3–8 breaks/unit per side) shed ~17–33% side-level — dissolution instead of cycling. The rationale is a PER-EVENT rate whose normal-battle aggregate matches the literature; the Shiloh per-battle aggregate is NOT read directly as a per-event number (§4 correction 1).
- **SL-2 · ENVELOPMENT-SURRENDER.** While `state==="routing"`: the unit's flight lane is the constant-x ray toward `fldHomeEdgeZ(u.side)` (T0:588-589). A **blocker** is an enemy unit with `state==="steady"` (the existing state enum — no new men-floor constant) whose x lies within a RALLY_R-class lateral half-band of the lane AND whose z lies **strictly between** the router's z and the home edge (the DIRECTIONAL clause — a pursuer behind the router never blocks). The corridor is **blocked** when ≥1 blocker exists AND no friendly steady formation is nearer to the router than the nearest blocker (the rescue clause). While blocked: `surrenderT += dt` and **`rallyT` does NOT accrue — blocked ≠ safe** (the rally-collision fix; when the corridor clears, surrenderT resets and normal rally resumes). At `surrenderT ≥ RALLY_SECS` (6s, reused; no leader modifier v1 — decide-&-log: fewest terms) the unit surrenders: its men bank to `__FIELD.captured[side]`, then `alive=false, men=0, state="captured"` (a `fldSurrender(u)` sibling of `fldKill`), announce + prisoner marker. Batteries surrender the same way; their `guns` are logged as captured guns in the after-action. Cavalry gets NO exemption (one rule — the mobility-fidelity debt is this line's documented compromise). No pathfinding, no RNG, O(N) scans mirroring the rally-danger scan (T0:529-533).
- **SL-3 · SCOPE GUARD.** Both mechanics fire ONLY when `__FIELD.attacker` is set (asymmetric scenario battles, incl. phased and campaign-linked). The symmetric sandbox (`attacker` null) is byte-identical — the E48 `holdLive` / `fldGoneCheck` precedent. Within asymmetric battles the rules are UNIVERSAL: baselines move wherever routs occur; there is no per-battle switch of any kind.
- **SL-4 · ACCOUNTING (subset-ledger law).** `battleCas` semantics are UNCHANGED: total men lost = fielded − survivors (T8:108-113) — shed and captured men flow in automatically with zero new wiring. `__FIELD.captured = {US,CS}` and `__FIELD.missing = {US,CS}` are **labeled SUBSETS for reporting, never added to battleCas** (the double-count guard). Killed/wounded is DERIVED: `cas − captured − missing`. Both ledgers reset in `fldResetRun`; T8 accumulates cumulative `battleCaptured`/`battleMissing` beside `battleCas` per phase. **The identity `fielded = survivors + killed/wounded + captured + missing` is a mandatory probe tooth.** T2 campaign fractions are UNCHANGED v1 (total-loss based — the OR convention: documented casualty bands include captured/missing, so A/B ratios stay comparable to sources). Straggler-recovery and POW-exchange wiring (captured men eroding enemyWill/manpower differently; the D161 exchange system) are EXPLICITLY DEFERRED to the post-E49 PM2 rebuild decision set.
- **SL-5 · SYMMETRY (§27).** Identical rules for player and AI units at every difficulty tier. The existing B-5 rout-save cushions stay upstream (fewer player routs at Recruit → less exposure) — difficulty expressed through accurate inputs, never a new cheat constant.
- **SL-6 · NO NEW RNG.** Neither mechanic draws from `fldRng`. Shed fires on the deterministic rout event; surrender on deterministic geometry + timer. The seeded stream is untouched → A/B and byte-comparability legs stay valid.
- **SL-7 · NO BACK-SOLVING (D74 extended).** f and every geometry term (band width, grace) are universal AND chosen from documented rationale — **never tuned to land any battle's casualty ratio or band** (target-tuning a universal constant is D74 outcome-engineering laundered through universality). Casualty bands are LOGGED, never gated; an honest under/overshoot is a divergence note, not a tuning license.
- **SL-8 · PRESENTATION.** Surrender announce ("X surrenders — N men captured"); a prisoner marker at the yield point (reduceMotion-safe, CVD-safe, not color-alone); after-action + interphase cards + end screens report killed/wounded · captured · missing columns; battle cards narrate the documented mass captures where sourced (Hornets' Nest ~2,200, Gettysburg Day-1 ~3,600, the Sunken Road ~300 — citation-stamped) once their flips land.
- **SL-9 · EDGE-CASE RULINGS (panel-verified).** Officers (`__FIELD.leaders`) and supply trains (`__FIELD.trains`) live outside `__FIELD.units` → excluded by construction. The predicate is **fog-blind** (ground truth — consistent with the rally/objective/gone scans, which never call fldVisible). Reinforcement spawns are safe (not routing on arrival); `surrenderT` initializes 0 at spawn and resets in `fldResetRun` (determinism). Phased battles inherit per-phase `homeEdge` via the existing `_fldBuildPhase → fldHomeEdgeSpec` route. Mass surrender MAY trip `fldGoneCheck` → `by=destroy` (desired at the Hornets' Nest class); the 9-battle sweep must show it bounded elsewhere. E48 interaction is structurally safe (panel-confirmed: the timeout transition is terminal, holdLive cannot oscillate; surrendered defenders during overtime accelerate an honest hold).
- **SL-10 · E53 STAYS OUT.** Assault-abandonment (the attacker calling off a failed assault — the D254 3.59 overshoot evidence) is FILED as ledger E53, designed after E49 + the E47 flips land. E49 must NOT touch the D64 attacker-commit logic (`effLocal`, T0:829-830).
