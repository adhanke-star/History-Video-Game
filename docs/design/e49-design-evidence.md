## 1 · The gap in one paragraph
The universal model has **no way for a broken force to be permanently lost short of death**: a routing unit flees toward its own home edge at SPD_ROUT, rallies after 6 safe seconds, returns at morale ≥30, and can repeat this indefinitely with zero man-loss. Real Civil War defeats ran on two missing mechanisms — **mass capture by encirclement** (Prentiss's ~2,200 at the Hornets' Nest; ~3,600 Union prisoners in Gettysburg's Day-1 retreat; the Sunken-Road lane's ~300 as Barlow enfiladed) and **straggler dissolution** (the thousands under the bluff at Pittsburg Landing who never returned). Five independent measurements now show the same fingerprint: defenses that historically dissolved are bottomless in the engine, and any change that makes a defender MORE brittle paradoxically makes it HARDER to beat.

## 3 · The engine facts the design must work with (code-verified this session, T0-field-sandbox.js)
- **Rout entry (T0:549-554):** `morale < ROUT_THRESH(18) − xp·1.5` → save roll (0.5 base + leader/cushion mods, capped 0.95) → `state="routing"`, `routEverCount++`. No man-loss on the event.
- **Rout movement (T0:589):** flees toward `fldHomeEdgeZ(side)` at SPD_ROUT 62 (faster than a line marches — pursuit can't catch it; E47 made the direction role-aware per scenario).
- **Rally (T0:527-545):** no enemy within RALLY_R 240 → `rallyT` accrues → after RALLY_SECS 6 (÷ leader bonus) the unit becomes "wavering" at `morale = max(morale, 30)`, rallyT 0. **Unbounded repetitions, zero attrition, full men retained.**
- **Recovery (T0:515-517):** MOR_RECOVER 2.2/s toward maxMor when safe (the D249 launch-debuff washout; E51's raidSupply finding rides this).
- **Attacker commit (T0:829-830):** `effLocal = ATK_LOCAL_RATIO / aiSkill × clamp(1/globRatio, 0.75, 1.4)`; a unit presses only when it locally out-masses the foe — so enemy-strength perturbations reach the attacker AI **non-monotonically** (the D251 corollary), and a never-dissolving defense converts the attacker into its own rout-rally grinder.
- **Casualty accounting:** `fldKill` men-decrement + `battleCas`/`_fldSidePhaseTally` (T8, D247) + the campaign fractions via `fldCampaignComputeOutcome` (T2). There is NO "captured/missing" class anywhere — a surrender mechanic must add one honestly (the OR casualty convention is killed/wounded/**missing** — the game's after-action can finally report the third column).

## 4 · The two APPROVED mechanics (D242 popup) — candidate shapes, zero per-battle constants (D74)
**(i) Envelopment-surrender.** A routing unit whose corridor to its own home edge is blocked by enemy formations surrenders: removed from the field, counted **captured** (a new casualty class the after-action reports separately). Candidate predicate: while `state==="routing"`, test the ray/lane from the unit toward `fldHomeEdgeZ(side)` — if steady enemy formations (men above some floor) sit within a lateral band across it and no friendly steady formation is nearer than they are, the unit surrenders after a short grace (reuse RALLY_SECS-class timing, no new constant families). Cheap deterministic geometry, no pathfinding.
**(ii) Straggler-shedding.** Every rout EVENT permanently sheds a fraction of the unit's men to a "missing/straggler" pool (they rally short). Natural compounding: a unit that breaks N times retains (1−f)^N — brittleness finally converts into dissolution, which is precisely what flips the D249/D251 inversion (at the traced 40-47 rout events, even f=0.05 leaves ~10% — the defense dissolves instead of cycling; at the base 11-14 events the cost is modest).
**Why BOTH (D242's finding):** surrender models Prentiss/Day-1 (encirclement, needs geometry); shedding models the bluff stragglers (no encirclement required). One rule each, both universal, both parameter-light.
