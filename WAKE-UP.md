**⚔ (prior firing) — PHASE B-3 · IN-BATTLE LOGISTICS on the tactical field:** the real-time battle gains an **ammunition economy**. Each side now fields a rear **ammunition train** with a finite battle reserve; a brigade that breaks off the firing line and reaches its train **refills its cartridge boxes** (drawing the reserve down), and when the reserve runs dry the army "runs low" — the real decider behind the 20th Maine's bayonet charge at Little Round Top. A brigade out of cartridges while holding the contested ground **fires dry, then goes to the bayonet** rather than abandoning the hill; a brigade pushed past exhaustion can barely drag itself forward. The **attacker's train sits far behind its start line** (it outruns its supply — Clausewitz's culminating point) and the **defender's just behind the objective it holds**, and the whole reserve **scales with the supply you built on the President's Desk** (a raid order cuts the enemy's) — so the logistics you win strategically pay off on the field.

**The bug-hunt earned its keep — and turned up a teaching-breaker.** A 64-agent adversarial hunt found that with **Fog of War on**, the new ammunition economy *inverted* one of the game's core lessons — "fog aids the defender" (the Henry House Hill lesson). At First Bull Run with fog on, the Confederate defender went from winning every AI-vs-AI game to *losing* every one: under fog the battle becomes a long fire-trade, and the attacker could cycle fresh, resupplied brigades to its safe rear train and simply out-shoot the pinned defender. Because it **contradicted a shipped teaching, I stopped and surfaced it to you** rather than silently patching it. Your first instinct (let the defender refill on its own depot) turned out to do nothing — the defender wasn't the side running dry — so we went with the fix that works: **an attacker advancing blind can't run its trains forward, so it fights with what it carries.** The result is clean: logistics is now **balance-neutral with fog either way** (Confederate 5/8 fog-off, 8/8 fog-on — each matching the no-logistics baseline), and a regression-guard test locks the fog case so it can never silently re-invert.
