S1e: Paths to Victory — make the South's collapse performance-driven, not scripted

Design-law correction (Aaron): S1c/S1d keyed the Confederate death spiral to the
CALENDAR (replacement ratio by year, recognition foreclosed at 1863, ports fall on
schedule), which made Southern defeat feel inevitable. The law is the opposite —
§5 deterministic BY PERFORMANCE (history is the favored "par", not a rail); §8 the
South wins by NOT losing; §29 a fair sandbox. Now WIN and the spiral does not happen.

src/80-victory.js + C.strategy + vicMomentum(C) (a 0..1 war-fortunes signal from
win-rate, home-front will, political capital):
- desertion is now (1-momentum)^1.6 -> a WINNING South barely bleeds deserters
- the CS replacement ratio's year curve is a "if you are losing" FLOOR that momentum
  lifts toward full replacement; arming the enslaved raises it further
- runner ports hold, and recognition does NOT foreclose, for a winning/courting South
- importFactor responds to runner investment + fortified ports

Executable Southern avenues (the counter-game) on a new "Paths to Victory" desk tab:
- Break Northern Will -> Negotiated Peace (the primary path; tracks the enemy's will,
  which winning erodes -> a victory becomes reachable)
- Foreign Recognition (kept open by winning or active courting)
- counter-blockade levers: invest in runners, commission commerce raiders, fortify
  ports; pursue recognition; deserter amnesty; arm-and-free the enslaved (Cleburne)
- wild card: provoke the Trent crisis (Anglo-American war) — alt-history (§5)

Refactored 60-blockade.js + 70-manpower.js to read momentum + the levers; registered
vicInit (early) + vicOnResolve (last) in the tick; 9th desk tab.

Counter-levers raise the lifeline; arm-the-enslaved surges the manpool. Full
